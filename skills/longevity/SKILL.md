## Essential Longevity Habits

### Diet & Exercise
- **Eat mostly plants, most of the time** — fruits, vegetables, beans, lentils, nuts, whole grains
- **Avoid processed and packaged food** — even "healthy" ones full of hidden oils and sugars
- **Eat legumes** — rich in plant protein, fiber, slow-burning carbs
- **Incorporate low-level activity throughout the day** — centenarians in Blue Zones rarely visit gyms but stay constantly active
- **Restrict calories periodically** — long-term calorie restriction is associated with slowed aging and reduced disease risk

### Lifestyle & Spirit
1. **Don't smoke** — including secondhand smoke. Be ruthless about air quality.
2. **Prioritize social engagement** — pros of being with people outweigh cons of disrupted routines
3. **Possess a strong life purpose** — NIH study: people with clear purpose lived longer
4. **Maintain low stress** — chronic stress = chronic inflammation = foundation of age-related disease
5. **Engage in spiritual practice** — 258 of 263 centenarians in Blue Zones belong to a faith community
6. **Stay reproductively useful** — signal to your body/brain that you're a valuable, contributing member

## Anti-Aging Tips from Experts

1. **Eat real food** (Charles Eugster) — Variety, lots of meat, especially fat. No zero-fat yogurt.
2. **Learn new stuff** (Laird Hamilton) — Force brain and muscles to maintain and build new neurons
3. **Lift, move, sprint** (Mark Sisson) — Short all-out sprints once every 7-10 days, not chronic cardio

## Recovery Intelligence

### 9 Signs You Haven't Recovered

1. **Sleep trouble** — tossing, early waking, or oversleeping
2. **Dark urine** — should be pale to slightly yellow
3. **Lost appetite** — decreases with under-recovery
4. **Elevated resting heart rate** — compare to your normal baseline
5. **Persistent muscle soreness** — should resolve within 48 hours
6. **Low energy** — distinguish true tiredness from need for rest
7. **Sudden weight loss** — 2%+ acute body mass loss affects performance
8. **Declining performance** — underperformance for multiple sessions
9. **Your own indicator** — identify what signal YOUR body gives you

### Heat vs Cold Therapy

| | Heat | Cold |
|---|------|------|
| **Does** | Increases blood flow, reduces stiffness/cramps | Numbs pain, reduces swelling/inflammation |
| **Best for** | Chronic pain, sore muscles, stress, joint pain | Acute injuries, fresh pulls, new inflammation |
| **Example** | Back pain, shoulder/neck pain | Freshly pulled muscle, IT band syndrome |
| **Timing** | NOT in first 48 hours after injury | Most effective early, first 48 hours |
| **Duration** | Max 20 minutes at a time | Max 20 minutes, let skin return to normal |

**Rule:** Ice is for injuries. Heat is for muscles. Never add heat to fresh inflammation.

## Breathing for Performance

**Diaphragmatic breathing (deep belly breathing):**
- Belly rises and falls with each breath (like babies breathe)
- Activates parasympathetic nervous system (rest mechanism)
- Benefits: improved circulation, calmed nerves, boosted immunity, increased breath capacity
- Use before training, before competitions, and whenever you feel stress

**Long exhale breathing:**
- Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat
- Lengthen inhale and exhale to equal length
- Progressively increase exhale until 1:2 ratio (inhale:exhale)
- 5-10 minutes — excellent for improving sleep

**Underwater breath hold (doubled in 3 days):**
1. 60-90 seconds of deep breathing (inhale through mouth, hold 1 sec, exhale 10 sec)
2. Purge extra CO2 with strong exhalations
3. Inhale normally (don't over-inflate)
4. Hold as long as possible — stay calm, divert your mind

## Travel Fitness

- **Keep exercise on the priority list** — add it to your calendar, set reminders
- **Make reasonable food choices** — steamed/roasted/broiled, skip fried
- **Use intermittent fasting** if you overeat — wait until 10am-noon for first meal
- **Hotel workouts:** Push exercise + pull exercise + leg exercise + core = 20 minutes
- **Carry tools:** Skipping rope, resistance band, tennis ball
- **Jet lag recovery:** Sunlight, walk barefoot on grass, avoid alcohol/caffeine on flight, drink OJ, hang upside down 5 min in the morning

## Healthy Habits Research (Harvard Study)

Five habits for living 12-14 extra years (Harvard, 73,000+ women, 38,000+ men):
1. Healthy diet (vegetables, fruits, nuts, whole grains, healthy fats, omega-3s)
2. 30+ minutes moderate-vigorous activity daily
3. Normal BMI (18.5-24.9)
4. Never smoking
5. Low-risk alcohol (women: 5-15g/day, men: 5-30g/day)

Even one habit = 2 extra years. All five by age 50 = **14 extra years for women, 12 for men.**

## Anti-Patterns

- Chronic overtraining without adequate recovery

- Ignoring mental health and emotional stress as causes of physical decline
