## The Core Insight: Healthspan, Not Just Lifespan

The goal isn't to simply recover from setbacks — it's to build a longer, healthier *health-span*: the years you spend feeling strong, sharp, and capable, not just alive.

**Your body is an operating system.** Like any OS, it needs:
- Regular maintenance (exercise, sleep, nutrition)
- Security patches (health screenings, blood work)
- Optimization (habits, supplements, practices)
- Upgrades (learning, adapting, improving)

Ignore maintenance and the system degrades. Optimize consistently and it runs better with age.

**We are living in an age of unprecedented progress in health and longevity.** The future is not about curing diseases — it's about preventing them in the first place. You have more tools, data, and knowledge available than any generation before you.

## The Trio of Total Health

Develop all three concurrently. Neglecting any one limits your everyday quality of life.

| Pillar | What It Means | Test |
|--------|--------------|------|
| **Strength** | Can you carry, lift, push, and pull your own body and real-world loads? | Squat bodyweight, bench 100kg, 5 pull-ups |
| **Speed / Cardio** | Can your heart and lungs sustain effort and recover quickly? | Run a mile in under 10 min; VO2 max |
| **Endurance** | Can you sustain effort and functional capacity over time? | Farmer carry bodyweight for 2 min; dead hang 1 min |

## Fitness Benchmarks: Measurable Tests of Progress

These are not gym PRs — they are longevity and functional fitness markers:

| Benchmark | Why It Matters |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Run 1 mile in under 10 minutes** | Cardiovascular fitness baseline |
| **Squat your own bodyweight** | Lower body strength and joint health |
| **25 unbroken push-ups** | Upper body strength and endurance |
| **5 unbroken chin-ups / pull-ups** | Upper body pulling strength |
| **Bench press over 100 kg** | Upper body pushing strength |
| **Farmer carry bodyweight for 2 minutes** | Grip strength and total-body endurance |
| **Dead hang for 1 minute** | Grip and shoulder integrity |
| **Sit-and-raise test (unassisted)** | Research-backed predictor of longevity |
| **Strong VO2 max for your age** | The single best predictor of long-term health |

*Track these annually. Progress is your signal. Regression is your warning.*

## Manjeet's 7-Step Longevity Blueprint

1. **Sleep** — When sleep is right, life is right. 7–8 hours, wake naturally without an alarm.
2. **Exercise often** — Best intervention for everything: mood, cognition, longevity, metabolic health.
3. **Hydrate and eat a Mediterranean-style diet** — Real, fiber-rich, protein-rich foods. Minimize processed food.
4. **Avoid self-harming behaviors** — Stop over-eating, over-drinking, over-thinking.
5. **Build atomic habits** — Align your daily actions with who you want to become.
6. **Monitor and track** — Get blood work done annually. Compare year-over-year. Measure to manage.
7. **Eat consistently** — A controlled Mediterranean-style diet with the same core foods daily maintains results. Monotony is part of the deal.

## Blood Work: Markers to Watch

Get a comprehensive panel annually. Flag and actively work to improve:

| Marker | Flag | Target Direction |
|--------|------|-----------------|
| **LDL cholesterol** | >100 (above optimal) | Reduce via diet and exercise |
| **Vitamin D** | 28.5 ng/mL (insufficiency) | Supplement to 40–60 ng/mL |
| **eGFR** | 83 (mild decrease) | Protect kidneys: hydrate, limit NSAIDs |
| **Eosinophils %** | High | Investigate allergies or inflammation |
| **Globulin** | Low; A/G ratio high | Review protein intake and liver health |
| **ALP** | Slightly low | Monitor, discuss with physician |
| **Plateletcrit (PCT)** | Low | Track, rule out deficiencies |
| **Fasting blood sugar** | Target <100 mg/dL | Diet, exercise, reduce sugar |

*Your numbers are data, not destiny. Most can be moved significantly through lifestyle.*

## The Supplement Stack

Layer these in order of evidence and safety:

### Foundations (High Evidence)
- **Protein + Creatine** — Muscle synthesis, strength, cognitive benefits. Non-negotiable for active adults.
- **Collagen** — Joint and connective tissue health, especially relevant after 35.
- **Vitamin D3 (5,000 IU) + K2 (100 mcg)** — Deficiency is extremely common; D3 alone may displace K2-dependent proteins without K2 co-supplementation.
- **Daily multivitamin** — Fill micronutrient gaps from diet.
- **Zinc + Curcumin** — Anti-inflammatory support.

### Longevity-Focused (Emerging Evidence)
- **NMN (1g/day, sublingual)** — NAD+ precursor linked to cellular energy and DNA repair. Sublingual delivery improves bioavailability.
- **Resveratrol (1g/day)** — Sirtuin activator; take with fat for absorption.
- **Trimethylglycine / TMG** — Methyl donor; often paired with NMN to prevent methyl depletion.
- **Metformin (2g/day)** — Requires prescription; consult your physician. Significant longevity research underway.

### Daily Non-Supplements Worth Treating Like Supplements
- **Sunscreen (daily)** — Look for 3% Avobenzone for anti-aging purposes. Apply even on non-beach days.
- **Sleep** — More powerful than any supplement.
- **Intermittent fasting** — Restricting eating to an 8-hour window is one of the most impactful metabolic interventions available.

## Anti-Patterns

- Treating health as something to fix *after* it breaks, not something to optimize *before* it does
- Optimizing for aesthetics only — appearance without function degrades quickly past 40
- Taking longevity supplements without fixing sleep, diet, and exercise first (supplements are multipliers, not foundations)
- Chasing intensity over consistency — two years of showing up beats one great month
- Ignoring blood work — your numbers tell the story your mirror can't
- Using busyness as an excuse — 20 minutes of movement is available to nearly everyone
- Scrolling and consuming alone as your default leisure mode — this is the slowest energy drain
- Drinking alcohol regularly by yourself (social is different from habitual solo drinking)
